About me

I am an 18 year old software developer from Pleasanton, California. I fell in love with technology at a young age, learning my first programming language, Lua, in eighth grade. From there I went on to learn other languages such as Java, Python, C++, and C either in the classroom or on my own free time.

During the summer of 2014 I attended Cornell University's Summer College where I took two courses, ENGRG1060 (Exploration in Engineering) and CS1100 (Introduction to Computing Using Python). I gained a good sense of how a product's development process functions, and learned a new programming language that I continue to use daily. I recently graduated Foothill High School and am currently studying computer science at University of Southern California in Los Angeles. I am currently taking a C++ course (CSCI103) as well as a general computing course (CSCI109).

I had the opportunity to work as an intern in Symantec Corporation's Data Loss Prevention group in San Francisco, California. During my time at Symantec, I worked on many projects, and worked with tools such as automation frameworks, Perforce, Jenkins, and Unix. I delved into cloud computing by working closely with the SaaS team to deploy the product in the cloud. Overall, I learned many useful skills in the field of software development during my six month tenure. Currently, I am a digital producer for USC's premier newspaper, the Daily Trojan. At the Trojan, I will be developing for their website and their mobile application. I cannot wait to see what future experiences in STEM have in store for me.
